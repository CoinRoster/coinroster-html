CoinRoster FAQ

What does CoinRoster do with users' BitCoins?
A very small percentage of our users' BitCoins are kept in "live" wallets hosted at CoinBase. This allows us to handle day-to-day deposits and withdrawals in a timely manner. The rest of the BitCoins are kept in offline "cold storage" wallets.

Our #1 security measure at CoinRoster is that there is no software link between the website and the actual bitcoins. We maintain a mirrored set of books offline. If someone were to hack CoinRoster, they could make an absolute mess of our site for a few days - but our users' bitcoins would be safe.


How much personal info do I need to provide?
None. All we need is a username, password, and your public wallet address. We recommend adding an email so that you receive records of your account activity. Also, without a verified email address you will not be able to recover your account if you lose your password.


What are RosterCoins (RC)?
RosterCoins are an internal currency that we use to award bonuses to players. 1 RC is worth 1 BTC, but RC can only be used to enter contests. For example, if you have 0.5 BTC and 0.5 RC and a contest entry requires 1 BTC, you can enter the contest.
