Connected Wallet Security
To make deposits and withdrawals to/from your CoinRoster account, we need to have a public wallet address on file for you. You can only have one address connected at a time, but you can change your address in certain circumstances.

Default security setting
By default, you can change your connected wallet address if and only if your Bitcoin balance is 0.

If we were to let you change your wallet address while your account had Bitcoins in it, a hacker could:

- steal your password
- log in
- change your wallet address
- request a withdrawal

Disabling security
After connecting a wallet you will have the option to disable this security measure, which will enable you to change your connected wallet address at any time.

You can disable security if and only if your Bitcoin balance is 0.

If we were to let you disable security while your account had Bitcoins in it, a hacker would now have one extra step:

- steal your password
- log in
- disable security
- change your wallet address
- request a withdrawal

Re-enabling security
If you do decide to disable security, you can re-enable it at any time. If you have Bitcoins in your account when you re-enable security, you will NOT be able to disable it again until you have withdrawn all of your Bitcoins to your connected wallet.
